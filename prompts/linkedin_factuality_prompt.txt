## TASK

================================================================================
## OUTPUT CONTRACT
================================================================================

Format:
  - Plain text only — no markdown headings, no code fences.
  - Inline backtick formatting for Swift/Apple API names is acceptable where
    present in the draft; preserve it.
  - No URLs or hyperlinks embedded in the post body.
  - No references section or source attribution lines.

Length: match the draft length unless trimming is required to remove
unsupported claims. LinkedIn posts perform best under 1,300 characters;
do not pad to fill space.

Tone: professional, practical, and direct. Write for an audience of
intermediate to senior iOS and Apple platform engineers. Avoid generic
business-speak and consulting-deck language.

Structure — preserve or strengthen all of the following:
  - Strong hook (first 1–2 lines visible before "see more").
  - Clear logical flow through the body.
  - Concrete CTA (call to action) at the close.
  - White space / line breaks for LinkedIn readability.

================================================================================
## FACT-CHECKING RULES  (apply in this order)
================================================================================

1. ADMISSIBLE EVIDENCE
   A claim may be kept as-is only when it is:
   a) Directly supportable from the allowed references above, OR
   b) A stable Apple platform or Swift engineering fundamental that does not
      depend on a specific version, release date, or unreleased feature.

2. REMOVE WITHOUT REPLACEMENT
   Delete any claim that:
   - References a non-existent API, framework symbol, or Apple product feature.
   - Cites a specific version number, release date, WWDC session, or
     deployment figure that cannot be verified from the allowed references.
   - Describes a company action, partnership, product launch, outage, or
     public incident not confirmed in the allowed references.
   - Presents a benchmark, metric, percentage, or hard number without a
     source in the allowed references.
   - Is phrased as fact but is speculative, anecdotal, or hypothetical.

3. REWRITE TO CONSERVATIVE WORDING
   When a claim is directionally plausible but cannot be verified with
   certainty, rewrite using hedged qualitative language:
     "typically", "in most cases", "generally", "may", "can"
   Avoid: "always", "guaranteed", "Apple confirmed", "as of [version]",
   "was introduced in", "proven to".

4. PRESERVE CONCISION OVER COMPLETENESS
   If removing a claim leaves the post thin, condense rather than pad.
   A shorter, accurate post is always preferable to a longer, speculative one.
   Do not add new engineering claims to fill gaps left by removals.

5. DO NOT INTRODUCE NEW CLAIMS
   Do not add APIs, metrics, version statements, or factual assertions not
   present in the original draft, unless they are direct restatements of
   the allowed references.

================================================================================
## STYLE RULES
================================================================================

Prohibited Phrases (remove or rewrite on sight):
  - "best practices"
  - "in today's fast-paced world"
  - "it is important to"
  - "leverage" (as a verb)
  - "game-changer"
  - "exciting"
  - "thrilled to share"
  - "I'm proud to announce"
  - Any other generic LinkedIn filler

Hook:
  The first 1–2 lines must earn the "see more" click. If the draft hook is
  weak, vague, or leads with a pleasantry, rewrite it to open with a concrete
  insight, a specific problem, or a direct engineering claim.

CTA:
  The closing CTA must be specific and actionable. Replace vague CTAs like
  "let me know your thoughts" with something tied to the post's engineering
  content (e.g., "What tradeoff has surprised you most when adopting X?").

================================================================================
## CONFLICT RESOLUTION
================================================================================

When requirements conflict, apply this priority order:
  1. Factual integrity — never assert unverifiable claims.
  2. Hook and CTA — both must be present and strong.
  3. Concision — stay within LinkedIn-appropriate length.
  4. Tone and style rules.
