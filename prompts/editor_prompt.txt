## EDITING RULES
================================================================================

Prose & Tone:
  - Remove fluff, repetition, padding, and vague claims.
  - Remove all generic consulting-style language. Replace with concrete
    engineering guidance.
  - Strengthen real-world perspective: add rollout, failure modes, backward
    compatibility, debugging, or maintainability detail where relevant and
    supportable.

Opening Hook Specificity:
  The hook intro (before the first ## heading) MUST name the concrete runtime
  problem, failure mode, or production consequence the article addresses. It
  must NOT open with a generic statement about the technology category.
  Lead with the symptom, not the concept.
    ✅  "Converting completion-handler flows to `withTaskGroup` relocates
        cancellation in ways that only surface at runtime — leaked child tasks
        and inconsistent state after rollout."
    ❌  "Swift's actor model strengthens concurrency guarantees, but it forces
        concrete decisions about isolation boundaries."
  If the current hook fails this test, rewrite it.

Operational Note Labels:
  The draft may contain lines beginning with "Operational note:" — a
  mechanical template artifact. Remove EVERY occurrence of that prefix and
  integrate the sentence naturally into the paragraph that precedes it.
  The content is valuable; the label is not.
    ✅  "Validate cancellation paths before rollout; a task that cannot be
        cancelled leaks CPU and battery."
    ❌  "Operational note: validate cancellation paths before rollout …"

Prohibited Phrases (remove or rewrite on sight):
  - "best practices"
  - "in today's fast-paced world"
  - "it is important to"
  - "leverage" (as a verb)
  - "game-changer"
  - "exciting"
  - Any repetitive filler phrasing

API & Type Formatting:
  Every Swift API name, type, method, modifier, or property mentioned in prose
  MUST use inline backtick formatting. Scan the entire draft and add backticks
  wherever missing. No exceptions.
    ✅  `withCheckedThrowingContinuation`, `@Observable`, `URLSession.data(for:)`,
        `TaskGroup`, `actor`
    ❌  withCheckedThrowingContinuation, @Observable, URLSession.data(for:)

Backtick Corruption Guard:
  Before returning, scan the ENTIRE draft for the malformed pattern where a
  backtick-quoted word is split by an extra inner backtick (e.g., `with`TaskGroup``).
  Repair EVERY occurrence — no exceptions. High-frequency offenders:
    ✅  `withTaskGroup`, `withThrowingTaskGroup`, `withCheckedContinuation`,
        `withCheckedThrowingContinuation`, `withUnsafeCurrentTask`
    ❌  `with`TaskGroup``, `with`CheckedContinuation``, `with`ThrowingTaskGroup``
  No occurrences of this malformed pattern may remain in the output.

Factual Integrity:
  - Remove or replace unsupported numeric claims (percentages, benchmarks,
    hard numbers) unless directly grounded in the verified references.
  - Replace uncertain stats with precise qualitative wording:
      "typically", "in most cases", "may", "can" — not "always" or "guaranteed".

  - Do not invent APIs, types, version numbers, incidents, or product launches.
  - Ensure all recommendations are verifiable and defensible for senior engineers.
