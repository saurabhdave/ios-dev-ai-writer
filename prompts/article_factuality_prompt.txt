================================================================================
## FACT-CHECKING RULES  (apply in this order)
================================================================================

1. ADMISSIBLE EVIDENCE
   A claim may be kept as-is only when it is:
   a) Directly supportable from the allowed references above, OR
   b) A stable Apple platform engineering fundamental (e.g., documented
      memory-management rules, Swift language semantics, long-standing
      UIKit/SwiftUI behaviour) that does not depend on a specific version,
      release date, or unreleased feature.

2. REMOVE WITHOUT REPLACEMENT
   Delete any claim that:
   - References a non-existent API, type, modifier, or framework symbol.
   - Cites a specific version number, release date, or WWDC session that
     cannot be verified from the allowed references.
   - Describes a company action, product launch, outage, or public incident
     that is not confirmed in the allowed references.
   - Presents a benchmark or performance figure without a cited source
     in the allowed references.

3. REWRITE TO CONSERVATIVE WORDING
   When a claim is directionally plausible but cannot be verified with
   certainty, rewrite using hedged qualitative language:
   - "typically", "in most cases", "generally", "may", "can"
   - Avoid: "always", "guaranteed", "Apple confirmed", "as of [version]",
     "in [year]", "was introduced in"

4. PRESERVE STRUCTURE OVER COMPLETENESS
   If removing a claim leaves a section too thin to be useful:
   - Condense the section rather than padding it with invented content.
   - A shorter, accurate section is always preferable to a longer,
     speculative one.

5. DO NOT INTRODUCE NEW CLAIMS
   Do not add APIs, code patterns, version statements, or factual
   assertions that were not present in the original draft, unless they
   are direct restatements of the allowed references.

================================================================================
## CONFLICT RESOLUTION
================================================================================

When rules conflict, apply this priority order:
  1. Remove/rewrite hallucinations (Rules 2 & 3) — always takes precedence.
  2. Preserve section structure (Rule 4).
  3. Maintain audience-appropriate depth (Output Contract — Audience).
  4. Minimise diff from original draft.
